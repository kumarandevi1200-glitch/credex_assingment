# SpendLens GTM Strategy

## Exact Target User
Our target user is specifically the **Engineering Manager or CTO at a 5-30 person seed or Series A startup**. They have likely added 3 to 5 distinct AI subscriptions in the past 18 months—from Cursor and Copilot to ChatGPT and Claude—primarily because their team requested them. They are currently paying out of pocket or via a relatively unscrutinized company credit card. They wouldn't consider themselves "budget-conscious" about AI because the productivity gains are obvious, but they suffer from subscription sprawl. They click "upgrade" when an engineer asks, without evaluating if the base tier is sufficient or if redundant tools overlap.

## What They Google/Scroll Right Before Wanting This
- "Cursor vs GitHub Copilot 2025" (figuring out if they need both)
- "Is Claude Pro worth it for engineering teams?"
- "AI tools cost too much reddit"
- Scrolling Hacker News discussions titled "Ask HN: What's your AI stack?"
- Browsing r/ExperiencedDevs threads on tooling ROI

## Where They Hang Out Online
- **Subreddits**: r/ExperiencedDevs, r/cscareerquestions, and occasionally r/SaaS.
- **Hacker News**: Engaged in "Show HN" and infrastructure optimization posts.
- **Slacks/Discord**: Private founder and leadership communities like the On Deck alumni Slack, Ramen Club Slack, or YC founder forums.
- **X/Twitter**: Following pragmatist engineering voices like @swyx, @levelsio, @patio11, and @GergelyOrosz.

## First 100 Users in 30 Days ($0 Budget)
1. **The "Screenshot Flex" on Twitter**: Create 5 audits of famous/public companies based on their job postings (e.g., "OpenAI says they use X, here's their estimated overspend"). Tag pragmatist influencers.
2. **Hacker News "Show HN"**: Frame it exactly as an anti-AI-hype tool: "Show HN: I built a deterministic script that tells you if your AI subscriptions are redundant." Emphasize that it's just math, no fluff.
3. **The "Oops, we have both" wedge in Slack**: In founder Slacks, whenever someone asks "Cursor or Copilot?", drop a link to SpendLens with: "I built a quick calculator to see if you're bleeding cash by running both. Usually, teams overpay by ~$1k/yr if they do."

## The Unfair Distribution Channel
Credex already possesses a list of companies purchasing their discounted AI credits. The unfair advantage is **warm, B2B peer referrals**. When a company buys credits through Credex, SpendLens can give them a tailored, branded audit link to share with their investor portfolio or founder peer groups. Credex can offer these existing customers an additional 5% off their next credit invoice for every peer company that runs an audit and converts, essentially turning their current customer base into an affiliate network targeting the exact same demographic.
